Education
At Ohio State, I am studying Computer Science Engineering with a specialization in Artificial Intelligence. I enjoy both the theoretical side of computer science as well as the software-engineering focused side. Algorithms and math are generally where I feel most comfortable. Some of my favorite courses I have taken so far include:
  • CSE2321 - Foundations I - Discrete Structures: First in a 2-course sequence covering Graphs, Proofs, Set Theory, Complexity Analysis etc.
  • CSE3321 - Finite Automata and Formal Languages: An additional theory course for students who enjoyed the foundations sequence - a class on formal languages, computational models and ultimately computability theory and its associated mathematics
  • CSE5526 - Neural Networks: A low-level look at Neural Networks and their implementation, we get to implement full networks from scratch - no libraries. Neural Networks is probably the most interesting and engaging class I've taken at OSU.

While I enjoy theoretical computer science mostly, I feel I am a mature programmer and software designer. My education outside of class - through internships and personal study - has made me an excellent writer of clean, readable, maintainable code. The most valuable thing I took away from my Summer 2019 internship with John Deere was a practical sense of how and why writing beautiful code is necessary. As a grader for the CSE department, I have spent a lot of time looking at and debugging code written by students who are just learning to code for the first time. That experience has been valuable in improving my own skills.
