Definition
ESports, short for electronic sports, is a type of competitive video game played for spectators, usually by professional players. ESports are becoming increasingly popular through advances in both video game design, and sharing via the internet through "streaming" sites such as Twitch.tv - where spectators can watch players over the internet.
Source: Wojazer, 2018. <https://www.engadget.com/2018/07/19/esports-summer-overwatch-fortnite-league-legends-pro-gaming-schedule/>
History of ESports
The first video game competition took place in 1972 at Stanford University, the competitors played the Atari game Spacewar.
Source: Timetoast, 2014. <https://www.timetoast.com/timelines/esports-history>
Modern ESports
Esport nowadays are commonly organized as professional, systematic, and commercialize tournaments or leagues around the world. For example, the 2018 League of Legend World Championship, 34 teams from all over the world attended for the championship. The World Championship final garnered 20 Million viewers. That's about as many viewers as the MLB World Series final had on average.
