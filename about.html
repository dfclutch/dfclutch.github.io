Introduction:
The scale of weaponry available to today’s military superpowers is something never witnessed in human history. With constantly changing and improving technology, our ability as a species to inflict damage upon one another is astounding. Most of this development is due to the efforts of engineers, paid to design and implement these weapons. The goal of this paper is to argue that design, construction, and maintenance of modern, war-related weaponry is directly contradictory to the requirements of first fundamental canon of engineering ethics
